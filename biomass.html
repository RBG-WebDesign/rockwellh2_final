Solutions — Biomass Pathway

Turn Your Waste Into
Clean Energy.

Our biomass-to-hydrogen pathway takes organic waste materials — agricultural byproducts, food processing residuals, methane — and converts them into hydrogen gas through thermolysis steam reforming. You get clean energy from material you'd otherwise pay to dispose of, plus a valuable byproduct: biochar.

How It Works

Waste In. Energy & Fertilizer Out.

A closed-loop process that converts organic waste into hydrogen fuel and biochar — a valuable soil amendment.

1

Collect Biomass Feedstock

Organic waste from your operation — crop residue, food waste, manure, methane — is gathered as fuel for the system.

2

Thermolysis Steam Reforming

The biomass is processed at high temperature to release hydrogen gas. No combustion of fossil fuels is required.

3

Hydrogen Created & Stored

Pure hydrogen is captured, compressed, and stored in Power Bank units for on-demand use.

4

Biochar Byproduct

The process also produces biochar — a carbon-rich material used as soil fertilizer. Both an environmental benefit and an additional revenue stream.

5

Power On Demand

Stored hydrogen is converted to electricity through fuel cells, or used directly to fuel vehicles and equipment.

Why Biomass?

Two Revenue Streams from One Waste Stream

Material you currently pay to haul away becomes the feedstock for your energy system — and a source of valuable biochar fertilizer.

Value From Waste

Material you currently pay to haul away becomes the feedstock for your energy system. Turn a cost center into an asset.

Biochar Revenue

The byproduct is a high-quality soil amendment with a growing market. What was waste becomes two revenue streams: energy and fertilizer.

Methane Reduction

Converting organic waste to hydrogen prevents methane from entering the atmosphere — a greenhouse gas 80× more potent than CO₂ over 20 years.

Tax Credits & Grants

Biomass-to-hydrogen projects qualify for federal production tax credits and multiple state and federal grant programs.

Circular Economy

Your operation becomes a closed-loop system: waste in, energy and fertilizer out. Lower costs, lower emissions, stronger sustainability story.

Ideal For

Is Your Operation a Good Fit?

Biomass-to-hydrogen works best for facilities with consistent organic waste streams. If you're generating any of these, you have fuel for a hydrogen system.

  • Tomato, almond, and pistachio processors with high volumes of organic waste
  • Dairies producing manure and methane
  • Wineries with grape pomace and vine cuttings
  • Cities and municipalities managing food waste and green waste
  • Water treatment facilities with biogas recovery

Same Modular System, Different Fuel Source

The biomass pathway integrates with the same Power Bank storage and Power OnDemand fuel cell products used in our solar pathway. Your hydrogen ends up in the same storage tanks, powers the same equipment, and delivers the same reliable energy — regardless of how it was produced.

💡 You can combine pathways: solar during the day, biomass from your waste stream — all feeding a single hydrogen storage system.
